TuckerMoE: Tensor Decomposition for MoE (Part 1)

The Problem: Parameter Explosion

Standard MoE Expert Weight
\[W_e \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{in}} \times d_{\text{out}}}, \quad e = 1, \ldots, E\] \[\text{Total params} = E \times d_{\text{in}} \times d_{\text{out}}\]

Our Solution: Tucker Decomposition

Expert Weight Factorization
\[G_e = \sum_{r=1}^{r_1} U_{\text{expert}}[e, r] \cdot \mathcal{G}[r, :, :]\]
  • \(U_{\text{in}} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{in}} \times r_3}\) — shared input projection (compress)
  • \(\mathcal{G} \in \mathbb{R}^{r_1 \times r_3 \times r_2}\) — shared core tensor
  • \(U_{\text{expert}} \in \mathbb{R}^{E \times r_1}\) — expert selection coefficients
  • \(U_{\text{out}} \in \mathbb{R}^{r_2 \times d_{\text{out}}}\) — shared output projection
Key insight: Experts share a common latent structure (\(\mathcal{G}\)). Each expert \(e\) is merely a linear combination over the core's first mode, controlled by \(U_{\text{expert}}[e, :]\).
